Explore Kenya's Treasures

From iconic savannahs to pristine coastlines, discover the magic of Kenya.

Maasai Mara

World-renowned for the Great Migration, where millions of wildebeest and zebra thunder across the plains. A premier location for big cat sightings.

Fun Fact: The name 'Mara' means 'spotted' in the local Maasai language, referring to the landscape dotted with trees and shadows.

Tsavo East

One of Kenya's oldest and largest parks, known for its vast, open landscapes and large herds of elephants, often glowing red from the dust.

Fun Fact: It's famous for its "red elephants," which get their unique color from dusting themselves with the park's rich, red volcanic soil.

Tsavo West

A land of dramatic scenery, including volcanic lava flows, Mzima Springs with its underwater hippo viewing, and lush, hilly terrain.

Fun Fact: The Shetani Lava Flow's name means "devil" in Swahili, as locals believed it was the devil himself emerging from the earth.

Amboseli

Famous for its breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro and its large, free-ranging elephant herds that are among the most studied in the world.

Fun Fact: Amboseli was the setting for Ernest Hemingway's famous short story, "The Snows of Kilimanjaro."

Diani Beach

A stunning tropical paradise with brilliant white sands, turquoise waters, and lush greenery, perfect for relaxation and water sports.

Fun Fact: Diani is home to the Colobus Conservation, a sanctuary dedicated to protecting the rare and beautiful Angolan colobus monkey.

Lamu Island

A UNESCO World Heritage site where time seems to stand still. Explore the narrow streets of the old town, where donkeys are the main form of transport.

Fun Fact: Lamu is the oldest and best-preserved Swahili settlement in East Africa, with a unique culture blending African, Arab, and Indian influences.

Ol Pejeta Conservancy

A pioneer in wildlife conservation, this conservancy is a sanctuary for rhinos and chimpanzees, offering a unique and educational safari experience.

Fun Fact: It is home to the last two northern white rhinos in the world, Najin and Fatu, who are protected 24/7 by armed guards.

Mombasa

Kenya's historic coastal hub, a bustling port city with a rich history reflected in its Old Town, Fort Jesus, and vibrant blend of cultures.

Fun Fact: The iconic "Mombasa Tusks" on Moi Avenue were built to commemorate a visit by Queen Elizabeth in 1952, not for the ivory trade.

Malindi

A historic Swahili town that has evolved into a popular beach resort, known for its marine national park and rich Italian community influence.

Fun Fact: Explorer Vasco da Gama erected a pillar here in 1498 that still stands today, marking his last stop in Africa before sailing to India.
