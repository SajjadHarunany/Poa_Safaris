The Best Time to Visit the Maasai Mara for the Great Migration

Posted on July 17, 2024 by Poa Safaris Team

The Great Migration is one of nature's most awe-inspiring spectacles, and the Maasai Mara National Reserve is center stage for its most dramatic moments. But when is the best time to go? While the Mara offers incredible wildlife viewing year-round, timing is key if you want to witness the thundering herds of wildebeest and zebra.

The Peak Season: July to October

This is the prime time. The herds typically arrive in the Maasai Mara from the Serengeti in Tanzania starting in July. During these months, the plains are teeming with over a million wildebeest, zebra, and antelope. This is also when you have the best chance of seeing the dramatic river crossings at the Mara and Talek rivers, where crocodiles lie in wait.

  • July/August: The herds begin to arrive and the first river crossings occur.
  • September: Often considered the absolute peak, with the herds spread across the plains and frequent river crossings.
  • October: The herds are still abundant, but they begin to move south towards the Serengeti as the rains approach.

The "Green" Season: November to June

Don't discount the other months! The so-called "green season" has its own magic. The landscape is lush and green, there are fewer crowds, and accommodation prices can be lower. While the massive migration herds are absent, the Mara is still home to an incredible density of resident wildlife, including the big cats, elephants, giraffes, and hippos. It's also the best time for birdwatching.
